My Learning Plan

My goal is to land a job in front-end, back-end, or software engineering and keep growing in this career. I've already started this journey by studying in this field.
One of my strengths is curiosity I love learning new things and exploring how things work.It keeps me motivated, helps me ask questions, and pushes me to keep growing and improving in everything I do, especially in tech and coding. I don’t give up easily, I push myself and always try to prove that I can do it.
My limitation is communication. In group meetings or when I have to speak out loud, I often feel shy and not very confident. I sometimes worry that others might not understand me, especially because English is not my first language. But I’m working on building my confidence and improving my communication step by step.br>
During this program I want to build more confidence, especially when it comes to job interviews, networking, and communication in a professional environment.
I sometimes feel shy or unsure when speaking in groups or meeting new people, so I want to get more comfortable with that, so I can work well with others and grow both personally and professionally. These skills will help me feel more ready and confident.

How I will work productively and safely with others: First, I need to take care of myself. I’ll take breaks when I need to, and speak up if I’m feeling overwhelmed or need help and respect others and help create a safe and inclusive space for everyone.
Stay organised, manage my time well, and come prepared to meetings and group work. I’ll also be open to feedback and always try to improve.

What I will do when things start to build up: I’ll pause and check in with myself. If I feel stressed, Take a break, step away from the screen, and clear my head.
and remind myself to be kind to myself not everything goes to plan, and that’s okay. Struggles help us grow, and sometimes the problem just needs a fresh mind (or a snack!). I’ll break things down into smaller tasks and focus on one thing at at a time.
And if I still need help, talk to someone sometimes just saying it out loud makes a big difference.

I’m always open to feedback and I see asking for help as a way to grow. if I’m stuck I’ll reach out to facilitators, classmates, or look for online resources. I’ll be honest with myself about what I’m struggling with and remember that I don’t have to do this alone — learning is a shared journey.
I don’t expect anything specific because I believe the Facilitation team knows their role well. But I do hope for flexibility and understanding, because everyone learns differently. For example, I chose to study online because I know I work better on my own with a flexible but responsible routine. I also tend to focus better and study faster at night.

I’m currently studying full-time and do not have a part-time job or other big commitments. This means I’m fully available and focused on my learning during the course. I’ll manage my time well, take breaks when needed, and make sure to keep a healthy balance between study and rest. If anything changes or I have new commitments, I’ll communicate that early.
