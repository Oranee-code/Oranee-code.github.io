Identity, Values, and Strengths


Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

One important decision I made was to leave my job so I could study full-time. It was hard because I didn’t have much support, but I really wanted to change my life and do something I enjoy. I had to choose between staying in a safe job or following what I want.
Now, when I look back, I feel it was the right decision and I’m proud of it.


Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

My culture and the people around me taught me to be respectful, patient, and to always work hard. I’ve seen people do their best with very little,
and that made me grateful and motivated to always learn and improve. My values come from my family and community to be kind, humble, and never give up.


Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

My biggest strength is curiosity. I always want to learn new things and understand how they work. I also don’t give up easily.
These strengths help me stay motivated, even when things are hard. They will support me through my study and in my future career.


Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

One of my limitations is communication. I feel shy when I speak in a group, and I worry people won’t understand me, especially in English.
Also, sometimes I learn a bit slower, but I always get there in the end. I know this about myself, so I try to stay patient and keep improving.


Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

At a past job, I worked with someone who didn’t want to cooperate with the team. It made things harder for everyone. I stayed calm and focused on my own work. That helped a little, but it didn’t fix the problem. I was just trying to let it go, but I should have done better.
Now, I think I could have talked to them in a kind and respectful way or asked a team leader for help.
I’ve learned that good communication is better than staying quiet or avoiding the problem.
